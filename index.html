About The Summit

The Logos Connection presents a two-day event of empowerment and renewal for leaders. Enjoy sessions with cutting edge leaders and experts integrated with opportunities for refreshment and relaxation.

Join us for an unforgettable experience that will transform your leadership approach and equip you with practical tools to lead with health, purpose, and resilience.

Empowerment

Gain powerful insights and strategies from experienced leaders

Renewal

Rejuvenate your leadership passion and vision

Connection

Network with like-minded leaders and build valuable relationships

Event Schedule

Day 1: Friday, September 12, 2025

5:30 PM - 7:20 PM

Registration, Recreation, & Reception - Main Lobby

This time will be used for distribution of materials to pre-registrants and the intake process for walk-in registrants, networking opportunities during a reception, and opportunities for recreation (arcade style games, tabletop games, and chair massages).

7:30 PM - 8:45 PM

Evening Session 1: Leadership Burnout - Main Auditorium

Facilitators: Tiffany D. Howard, M.S. LCPC & Barry J. Elliott, MBA, MDiv

The focus of this session will be discussing the prevention and management (remediation) of leadership burnout, as well as the importance of taking guilt-free breaks, sabbaticals, or vacations.

7:30 PM - 8:45 PM

Evening Session 1B: Emerging Leaders - Lower Level

Facilitators: Canaan Grier & Malachi Thompson

This session targets ages sixteen through thirty years old who are on a leadership track or who are young adult leaders. The goal of this session is to emphasize core values such as authenticity, emotional intelligence, humility, and integrity.

9:00 PM - 10:00 PM

Evening Session 2: The Locker Room (Men) - Lower Level

Facilitator: Rashad Mills, MS, LCPC

The focus of this session is male empowerment to discuss matters relevant to men’s mental and spiritual wellbeing. This forum will provide a safe space for men to discuss the challenges of manhood and the balancing of being strong while not failing to be human.

9:00 PM - 10:00 PM

Evening Session 2B: The Lady’s Room (Women) - Main Auditorium

Facilitator: Dr. Karen Stallings, MS, LCPC

The focus of this session is women’s empowerment to discuss matter relevant to women’s mental and spiritual wellbeing. This forum will provide a safe space for women to discuss the challenges of womanhood and commitment to self-care.

Day 2: Saturday, September 13, 2025

8:30 AM - 9:15 AM

Registration, Recreation, and Reception - Main Lobby

This time will be used for distribution of materials to pre-registrants and the intake process for walk-in registrants, networking opportunities during a continental breakfast, and opportunities for recreation (arcade style games, tabletop games, and chair massages).

9:20 AM - 9:45 PM

The Chapel Experience - Main Auditorium

This time will be a time of prayer and worship.

9:45 AM - 11:00 AM

Morning Session 3: The Unmasked Leader - Main Auditorium

Facilitator: Dr. Peter Wherry, PhD

The focus of this session will be authentic leadership that lives out the principles of relatability, reliability, and courage. There will also be dialogue about leadership vulnerability (inclusive of honesty about doubts, fears, limitations, and struggles) and knowing when and when not to disclose such.

11:15 AM - 12:30 PM

Session 4: Guarding Your Influence - Main Auditorium

Facilitator: Dr. Alan G. Porter, PhD

The focus of this session will be the importance of protecting your personal brand, your leadership brand, and that of your organization. In a day of chronic leadership scandal, this session will provide tools for leading above reproach.

1:30 PM - 2:45 PM

Session 5: Master Class - Main Auditorium

Panelists: Dr. Alan G. Porter, Dr. Karen Stallings, Dr. Peter Wherry, Tiffany Howard, Rashad Mills, Barry J. Elliott (Facilitator)

The expert panel will share strategies for leadership longevity and building a sustainable leadership model. Additionally, the experts will share wisdom on navigating the task of leadership in a manner that is healthy, purposeful, and productive.
